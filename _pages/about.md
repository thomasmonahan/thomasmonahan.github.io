I am a Schmidt AI in Science Postdoctoral Fellow, Senior Research Associate, and
W.W. Spooner Junior Research Fellow at New College in the Department of
Engineering Science at the University of Oxford.

My research aims to enable reliable study and prediction of climate-driven ocean
change by learning how ocean systems respond to forcing from Earth observations.
I combine classical and next-generation altimetric and in-situ observations,
dynamical theory, and interpretable probabilistic machine learning to build
models that are scientifically informative, operationally useful, and directly
applicable as geophysical corrections for Earth-observation missions. My work
spans processes from coastal flooding to large-scale circulation, with a focus
on prediction, attribution, and uncertainty quantification.

I lead a research programme funded by a £211k Agile Initiative grant, working
with the Environment Agency, the National Oceanography Centre, and the Met
Office to operationalize my tool [RTide](https://github.com/thomasmonahan) for
storm surge forecasting in the UK. RTide has replaced the operational surge
forecasting system in the Netherlands and is being implemented at the Thames
Barrier, protecting over £400 billion in assets and 1.5 million people.

Research interests
======

- Physics-informed and probabilistic machine learning for ocean and coastal
  processes
- Satellite altimetry and Earth observation (SWOT, OSTST)
- Storm surge, tidal, and compound coastal flood forecasting
- Sea-level rise, the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation, and
  climate-driven ocean change
- Bayesian inference, uncertainty quantification, and symbolic regression

Prospective students & collaborators
======

I am always keen to hear from motivated students and researchers interested in
working at the intersection of ocean/coastal physics, Earth observation, and
machine learning. I currently supervise a team of eight researchers — a
postdoctoral researcher, four doctoral students (three through the Intelligent
Earth CDT), an MSc student, a 4th-year project student, and an undergraduate
intern.

**Prospective DPhil students:** If you are interested in pursuing a DPhil in
topics spanning probabilistic/physics-informed machine learning, satellite
altimetry, storm surge and tidal dynamics, or coastal flood forecasting, I
encourage you to look at the
[Intelligent Earth CDT](https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/courses/intelligent-earth)
and other DPhil routes through the Department of Engineering Science, and to
get in touch by email with a brief description of your background and research
interests.

**Prospective postdocs:** I am open to supporting applications for independent
fellowships (e.g. Schmidt AI in Science, Royal Society, NERC, Marie
Skłodowska-Curie). Please reach out with a CV and a short description of the
research you would like to pursue.

**MSc / 4th-year project students at Oxford:** Please get in touch if you would
like to discuss project ideas.
